Monarch Butterfly

Texas is an important state in monarch migration because it is situated between the principal breeding grounds in the north and the overwintering areas in Mexico. Monarchs enter the Texas portion of this flyway during the last days of September. By early November, most have passed through into Mexico. Seeking emerging milkweeds, they move through Texas laying eggs and the next generation of monarchs re-populate the eastern half of the US and southern Canada.

5 Ways You Can Help

PLANT MILKWEED

Planting milkweed in Texas is crucial for the survival of monarch butterflies, as it serves as the sole host plant for their caterpillars, providing essential nutrition for their development and supporting their life cycle.

NECTAR PLANTS

Texas residents can play a vital role in promoting pollination by cultivating nectar-rich plants native to the region, providing essential food sources for monarchs and other pollinators while beautifying local landscapes.

PESTICIDE FREE

Maintaining pesticide-free gardens and landscapes in Texas helps safeguard monarch butterflies and pollinators from harmful chemicals, ensuring a safer and more sustainable environment for these essential species.

SHOP AT LOCAL GARDEN CENTERS

Shopping at local garden centers in Texas not only supports the state's economy but also encourages the availability of native plants that are vital for monarch butterflies and pollinators, promoting biodiversity in local ecosystems.

PROTECT TEXAS PARKS

Protecting Texas parks and natural habitats is essential for preserving monarch butterfly populations, as these areas provide critical breeding grounds and migratory stopovers for these iconic insects, requiring conservation efforts to ensure their long-term survival.
